Timeline of Food System Shifts

1961

Rise of Industrial Agriculture — a global shift toward high-yield, chemical-intensive farming began.

1977

US Dietary Guidelines Introduced — early attempts to address national nutrition led to increased focus on carbs over fats.

1992

Factory Farming Boom — CAFOs and monocultures took over the food system to meet growing meat demand.

1996

Introduction of GMOs — genetically modified crops were first commercialized, aiming to improve yield and resistance.

2006

Meatless Monday Movement — launched in the U.S. to promote reduced meat consumption for health and sustainability.

2013

Lab-Grown Meat Debuts — the first lab-grown burger is publicly eaten, sparking global interest in cellular agriculture.

2023

Plant-Based Shift — rise of climate-conscious eating, mainstreaming of vegan alternatives.

2024

EU Approves Cultivated Meat Trials — the European Union greenlights pilot testing of cultivated meat for consumer markets.
