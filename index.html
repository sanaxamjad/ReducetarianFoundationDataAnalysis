Data Visualizations

This chart shows the global total agricultural production value from 1961 to 2014 using FAO data. It highlights a steady upward trend driven by population growth, technological advancements, and expanded agricultural practices, with a sharp spike around 1992 likely due to geopolitical shifts and improved reporting. Overall, it reflects rising demand and growing production capacity, offering insight into long-term trends in global food systems.

About the Project

This project was created by Sana Amjad as part of the Reducetarian Fellowship to raise awareness and inspire engagement through interactive learning tools. As a multidisciplinary technologist passionate about ethical technology and public interest design, Sana conceptualized, designed, and implemented this initiative using principles of human-centered computing, data storytelling, and inclusive design. Drawing from her background in Information Science, with a specialization in Data Science and minors in Risk Management and Tech Policy, she aimed to create a tool that is both educational and impactful, bridging the gap between awareness and action on key issues like food sustainability and climate justice. .
